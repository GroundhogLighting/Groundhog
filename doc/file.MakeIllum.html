Including illum surfaces in Groundhog

As was mentioned in the Getting Started section, illum surfaces are useful for rendering and modeling luminaires. Even though they are not an easy-to-understand concept, Greg Ward helped me define illum surfaces as:

Illum surfaces, also known as “impostors” or “proxies,” are light-emitting surfaces whose luminous distributions are equivalent to the distribution transmitted through them as the consequence of the existence of other (direct and indirect) light sources. Mkillum is the program that allows creating illum surfaces in a convenient way (Ward & Shakespeare, 1998). For example, the daylight distribution transmitted through a window, which can be considered a consequence of the existence of the sun and sky, can be summarized into an illum surface.

Although I have not used illum surfaces too much, I understand this might be a useful feature for some people.

It is important to notice that every time we make a window opening in Radiance, an illum surface is created on the interior side of the wall. However, there might be times when we want to create others. The only way of doing this is to Label a surface as an illum.
